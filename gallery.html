Morroco Cousine

Mokuda are Moroccan potato cakes or fritters. They are a popular street food in Morocco, where they might be eaten plain or used as a sandwich filler . They are also be served as a side or appartizer.
Cousous is a basically dish made usually of meat and vegetables cooked in a delicious broth served on a bed of steamed semolina granules.
Kefta tagine is a classic Morrocan tagine variety consisting of small meatballs in a homemade tomato sauce.The meatballs are made of lamb or beef. Sometimes kofta tagine also includes eggs, which are added to the sauce near the end of cooking.
